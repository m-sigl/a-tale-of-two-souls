2013 – Postcards and Positions

They travel light. Except for secrets. Those they carry everywhere.

To the world, they were just another retired couple chasing adventure. Blog updates. Smiling selfies. Postcards stamped from Athens, Kyoto, Tulum.

But behind the filters was something else. In the hotel rooms. On the trains. On rooftops they weren’t supposed to be on. They touched like newlyweds. Fucked like myth. Whispered names they only used abroad.

She had a list: places they hadn’t made love yet. It wasn’t written down — it was sacred. Etched behind her grin every time she handed him the keycard.

He brought the camera. The real one. The old one. No cloud backups. No auto-sync. Only prints, tucked between guidebooks and spare socks.

They left no trace. Not in metadata. Not online. But she kept a box in her suitcase: matchbooks, sand, receipts, rope. Memories not meant for show — only for skin.

They weren’t hiding. They were **protecting** it — what they’d found. Not just love, but play. Not just intimacy, but ritual. A private religion made of moans, museums, and midnight check-ins.

People said they were “goals.” They just smiled. And booked the next flight.
